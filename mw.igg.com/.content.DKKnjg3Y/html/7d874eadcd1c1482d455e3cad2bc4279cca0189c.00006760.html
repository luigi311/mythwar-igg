 agi makes you go faster in battle. actually, agi increases your speed, which in turn makes you go faster in battle. meaning during the same amount of time, if it's long enough, you will have more turns than another unit with lower speed. and of course that mean you will overlap the slower unit, at some points.

now, 200 speed doesn't translate into 2 times faster than 100 speed. 200 might overlap 100 speed, say, every 20 turns. 1000 speed might overlap 200 speed every 4-5 turns, commonly seen in agi humans overlapping their own slow tanks, missing pots in the process. the exact formula on how your speed affects the filling speed of your action guage is not known.

it's technically not "turn-based" like board games but "active turn-based" (ATB) like most final fantasy games and many others. the latter allows overlapping the former doesn't.

high speed also means you will be first to get hit by most spells (except poison and heal).

most party strategies generally don't take into account the overlapping. which is understandable because overlap will happen much further into the fight when it's already stablized. so, in term of strategizing, considering speed is only to determine who go first and who get hit.
