2.2: MW-Forums Interface




After successfully logging in, you will be redirected to the page http://forum.mw.igg.com/index.php. From there you can select which forum/sub-forum you wanna go.


On the top-left you see:



New Posts  -  link will list down all the latest posts (that includes replies).
Mark Read  -  link marks all the latest posts as read.

Threads with new posts in it will have a "new" animated image attached with the title; so clicking the "Mark Read" link will removed the "new" animated image.


On the top-right you see:



Today: [number]  -  Amount of new posts made today.

Yesterday: [number]  -  Amount of new posts made yesterday.

Highest: [number]  -  Most amount of posts made in 1 day ever.

Digest   -  This link will list down all digested post/threads.

Topics: [number]  -  Total amount of topics in the forum.

Posts: [number]  -  Total amount of posts in the forum.

Members: [number]  -  Total amount of forum members.

Welcome new member [member]  -  Newest forum member.

2.3: Posting


2.3.1: Creating a Post




There are 3 ways to create a new post/thread. One is clicking the “New” button found rightmost of the page (while looking at the topic list). See attachment sec.2.posting-1.
Second way is by clicking the “New” button found rightmost of the page (while looking at the forum post itself). See attachment sec.2.posting-2.
Another way is by using the “Fast Post” option located at the bottom of each forum post. See attachment sec.2.posting-3.

sec.2.posting-2


sec.2.posting-3




2.3.1.1: Posting Form




When create a new post or reply,you will be taken to a posting form for reply/new post.



Username  –   Your username, of course.

Subject  –  The subject/title of your post.

If you are creating a new post, subject field is required. If you’re just replying to a post, this field is optional. Also, when you create a new post, you can add tags on your post, depending on what type of post you are creating and on what forum it is created.

Icon  –  Icon of your post, the default is None. (see image below)



Message  –  The Message body is a large text area where you can type in your message.

Plain text is the only thing allowed in this text area, but special formatting,links, smilies (emoticons), images, etc. may be added through the use of Smilies,BBCode and/or HTML (if they are enabled).

Smilies  –  This is also called emoticons.

Smilies are the little faces to the left of the input for message; they display graphical faces instead of simply a :). You can enable/disable smilies by toggling “Disable Smilies” ON/OFF.

Use Signature  –  Enable it if you want your signature you have made in your Profile Settings to be automatically attached when creating/replying a post.

Email Notification  –  Enable this option if you want to watch your or other people’s post.

If anyone would reply on your post, an email notification will be sent regarding this.

Check Length  –  Pressing this button will count the character you typed in the message text area.

The allowed length to post a message is from 10 – 15000 characters/letters.

Preview Post  –  You can preview the post you are doing by pressing this button.

The preview will be showed at the top of the posting form. Please note that not all BBCodes will be viewed correctly in the Preview mode, for example this code;
Copy to clipboard
Code:
[quote] [/quote]
Reset Post – Resets or deletes all the info you have entered in the message text area.

Discuz! Code  –  Also known as BBCode. You can enable/disable Discuz! Code by toggling “Disable Discuz! Code” ON/OFF.
Quote:
So what’s BBCode?

BBCode is a special implementation of HTML. Whether you can actually use BBCode in your posts on the forum is determined by the administrator. In addition you can disable BBCode on a per post basis via the posting form. BBCode itself is similar in style to HTML, tags are enclosed in square brackets [ and ] rather than < and > and it offers greater control over what and how something is displayed. Depending on the template you are using you may find adding BBCode to your posts is made much easier through a clickable interface above the message area on the posting form. Even with this you may find the following guide useful.

2.3.2: Viewing a Post







2.3.2.1: Printable Version


If you want to print a certain post/thread Just click on the “Printable Option” link located right of the thread/post title.



2.3.2.2: Email to Friend


You can email a certain post/thread to a friend. Clicking this link will lead you to a page where you can type in your name, your email address, your friends name and email address and the message containing the link of the selected post.



2.3.2.3: Subscription


You can watch a certain post by clicking this link. Supposedly, every time there’s a new update/reply to your selected post/thread, you will receive an email notification regarding that, but email sending is not working. You can view all the threads/posts you have watched at the My Subscriptions page. As i noticed it, if the thread you have subscribed is not active within 24 hours, it will be deleted from your list.



2.3.2.4: Favorites


If you like a certain post/thread, you can put it on your favorite list so that you can view it anytime you want. View your favorites at My Favorites page. Note that only 99 posts/threads can be put in your favorites list.

2.3.4: Editing/Deleting your Post/Reply




Editing a post/reply:
Edit your post if you want to fix some errors, add more info, or remove incorrect information. To edit a post,click on the “Edit” link at the bottom right of your post.



You will be redirected to the posting form to edit your data, and then press “Edit Post” button to update your post. When you edit your post, a notification will appear at bottom of your post indicating when it was last edited.




Deleting a post/reply:
To Delete a post, click on the “Edit” link at the bottom right of your post. You will be redirected to the posting form to edit your data, check the “!Delete this Post” option and then press “Edit Post” button to delete your post/reply.

2.3.5: Attaching Image to a Post/Reply




There are two ways to attach an image to a post.

1. One is using the attach image button on the message formatting options.



Using this method,you will be prompted to enter the URL of your image, thus, you need to upload your image to http://www.photobucket.com/ or http://imageshack.us/. When you have entered your image URL, you will see the BBCode for your URL in the message box(if you’re on the Discuz!Code tab), if you’re on the WYSIWYG tab, you will immediately see the image you have attached.




2. Another way of attaching an image is using the “Uploading New Attachments” option just right below the posting form. See attachment.



Using this method, you can attach images directly from your PC. You can upload multiple files by pressing the [+] link. Note that the image you’re going to attach will be placed at the bottom of your post. You need to submit your post first before you can place the image in its proper place. Meaning, after you submitted your post, you can "edit" your post and place your uploaded files on the proper place.

2.3.7: Creating a Poll





To create a new Poll, mouse over the “New” button then select “Poll”.



You will be redirected to the posting form where you can input your data required for the Poll thread. Notice that there are new options added on the posting form applicable for making polls.

Reading Access (For Moderator, Super Moderators, Admins only)  – input data here if you want your poll to be limited for certain member status.

Member Status Reading Access:

Beggar - 0
Newbie – 10
Member – 20
Conqueror - 30
Lord – 50
King – 70
Forum Legend – 90

Subject  –  Title of your poll.
Valid days  –  put in how much time your poll will be open for voting. Leave it blank or zero for no limit.
Poll options  –  Enter here your poll choices; one choice per line.

Tick “Result visible after submit” check box if you want the graphical result visible after a user votes. Tick “Allow multiple choice” if you people have multiple choices.





2.3.7.1: Editing/Closing a Poll




Should you want to update/close your poll, just click on the “Edit” link, you will be redirected to the posting form where you can edit your poll.

1. To add new choice(s), just click on “Add Options” link and a new text field for the choices will appear.

2. To re-order the options’ positions, type in the order number beside the option text field.

3. To close your poll, just tick “Close Poll” check box then submit.

If you want to view the people who have voted in the poll, just click on the “Show Voters” link on the of the poll result graph.
