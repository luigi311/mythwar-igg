I like the idea of making devil tears and phoenix stones ingame items, but I don't see it happening. It's the company's way of keeping a regular flow of cash into the item mall, rather than people buying only luxuries items, they make practical items mall items- I don't expect to see any ingame events to give access to pet lifespan, except maybe one event they'll be willing to give an extremely rare drop of the '+500 pet lifespan' item.
When my favourite pets have run out of lifespan, I decided between leaving the game or buying a super phoenix stone. I chose to stay. I'm sure a lot of other people will make the decision the other way. Everyone has their own favourite pets, and the loss of those pets through running out of lifespan makes people f54 , f78 and f80 in regards to your game. It was a close decision for me if I should abandon this game or stay when this happened to me. I don't mind the lifespan feature, but I do hate that ONLY a mall item gives your pet back.

"Heal group" at the nurse is a good suggestion, as is ingame weekly or monthly events for silver ingots. The choice of items from silver ingots is very limited and I would think should be expanded.


Edit: I was being too harsh here, and speaking unreservedly without thought for where I was doing it. I apologise for any offence caused.

Update the website so that it is correct in the details that it gives. Fixing the website would include deleting most of the information on the marriage and brotherhood page. (As there is no brotherhood or couple's quest NPC, no brotherhood skill, and no marriage skill as examples.) I use this as an example, I am not saying this is all the flaws the website contains.

After playing MW1 for a long time with that blank ring slot in the inventory, I noted happily that MW2 had engagement rings to finally fill that slot. Ha. The rings can't actually be worn, but sit in the inventory. Make 'em wearable.

Mostly negative suggestions I've made, I know (fix this, fix that), but I think I'm being fair. What is wrong already should take precedence over adding new features. Many of the bug posts and alike problems have been known to be problems right from the beginning of MW2, and yet nothing has been done.
