since i'm first reply here. I think I would add some useful info regarding skills here:

you need <skill> I to lvl 25 before you can upgrade <skill> II freely, otherwise <skill> II lvl cannot exceed <skill> I lvl.
you need <skill> II to lvl 40 before you can upgrade <skill> III freely, otherwise <skill> III lvl cannot exceed <skill> II lvl.
you need <skill> III to lvl 50 before you can upgrade <skill> IV freely, otherwise <skill> IV lvl cannot exceed <skill> III lvl.

This applies to all individual skills. In other words, replace <skill> above with Death, Stun, Drain, Heal, etc. This has no effect across different skills, for example the level of Fire I has nothing to do with Flash II.

Simple Examples:

If you want Drain II lvl 20, you will need Drain I at least lvl 20. (20 < 25)
If you want Chaos II lvl 35, you will need Chaos I at least lvl 25. (35 > 25)

Complicated Examples:

If you want Frail III lvl 20, you will need Frail I at least lvl 20 and Frail II at least lvl 20. (20 < 25)
If you want Heal III lvl 35, you will need Heal I at least lvl 25 and Heal II at least lvl 35. (25 < 35 < 40)
If you want Speed III lvl 45, you will need Speed I at least lvl 25, and Speed II at least lvl 40. (35 > 40)

If you want Stun IV lvl 20, you will need Stun I at least lvl 20, Stun II at least lvl 20, and Stun III at least lvl 20. (20 < 25)
If you want Death IV lvl 35, you will need Death I at least lvl 25, Death II at least lvl 35, and Death III at least lvl 35. (25 < 35 < 40)
If you want Ice IV lvl 45, you will need Ice I at least lvl 25, Ice II at least lvl 40, and Ice III at least lvl 45. (40 < 45 < 50)
If you want Fire IV lvl 75, you will need Fire I at least lvl 25, Fire II at least lvl 40, and Fire III at least lvl 50. (75 > 50)
