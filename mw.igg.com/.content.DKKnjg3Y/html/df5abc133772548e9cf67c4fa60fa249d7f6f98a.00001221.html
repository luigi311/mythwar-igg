I Foreword

If you came here to find the uber-pwnzOr-human-build that kicks a$$ like hellz in every gw, then you're wrong here - get the hell out!  

If you came here to find a nicely balanced in detail pvm (player vs monster <-> pvp, player vs player)human guide that explains most things step by step which recommends this guide also for newbies - enjoy reading!  

All my considerations are pure pvm and therefore this guide if especially for female humans. While large passages of this guide's considerations also suit for male humans or are even interesting for other races, I always mean female human when I take things into consideration or discussing skillusage, usefullness, ...whatever.  


II Introduction

So you decided to play a human, but why should you?   
Let's have a look at the tasks a human has. A humans main task is to "decrase the ememy's possiblities to harm your party". So, if you want a damager - play mage or borg. If you want an allrounder - play cent. But a human will always be a specialist!  
Ok, what do I mean by "specialist"?  
I tell you that for a human it's more crucial then for any other race (no offence to you mages, cents and borgs out there) to know what you're doing and to act flexible according to the abilities of the rest of your party and the fighting situation. (If you think humans are all about speed and plainly chaosing: again, get out of here!)
And with being a specialist you will have to face several problems.  
One of them is finding a party to level. While (at least 1) human is very welcome in difficult quests (Papa Drow, Worn Diety, Guildwar, Hurricanes - up to 3 humans at 14), for each other quest (mainly defender / seal) one human is already more then enough.
So, how can we prevent not finding a party to level?  
1. by making/having friends (no guide for this - sorry guys)
2. by being a good human - read on.  

Dragonroost: "Having two humans that don't harmonize in party is worse then having no human in party."




III Step by step playing guide (for newbies)

Where to put stats?

My build 2 agi - 2 stamina. Best build for me, don't say others are not good, but discussion about builds in general if pretty philosophical and would nuke this guide.  
This build will provide (at level 104 naked) :
50k+ HP
26k+ MP
350+ Speed
(after reborn you can boost your speed up to nearly 1k anyway by chosing equipment and ssp - more then enough for pvm).  


Small leveling guide (from my own experience / preference)

If you "feel pro" - skip  
"Hint" - always play in party for more experience and always use double experience. Use your double experience wisely, because it's limited to 14 h / week / character
1-6 Space tunnel (kill with Poison I)
Get double Experience from Blyton Skill center
6-15 Flammigor (best comming from Revive Arena)
15 - get II skills in Woodlingor Skill Center (buy an apple if you fail to finish quest)
15-32 Devils Gate (kill with Poison II and collect some Nelson Boots you can sell later   )
32-36 lower Bone Desert (Wolves + Skeletons)
36-38 middle Bone Desert (Skeletons + Fogs)
38-5x upper Bone Desert (Fogs and Mudrapers)
50 - get Quest for Drowcrusher in Woodlingor Skill Center, buy +5 mail of the oceanic in Demon Square and ask some people nicely to help you beat Drowcrusher.  

Here you should stop and get mokka max / train your skills. Yes it's boring, but it makes your leveling later pretty much easier and faster because with good skill.  
In opposite to Mages who mostly do pretty well with one skill maxed, Humans have to rely on severaly different skills for different situations.
So, here is a small todo-list with skills you should train after you're mokka max.  
Must: Chaos III + IV level 9 (to hit 5 targets with it)
Optional: Poison II level 9 (for speeding up outside quest leveling)

Leveling continues... with 2 alternative routes

Defender Route
5x-60 - Woodlingor Defender
60-70 - Blyton Defender
70-91 - Seal

If you do this you might want to take a look at this guide.

Alternative Route (strongly recommended if you catch a 4x experience event)
57-60 Ashes Pit 1
60-62 Ashes Pit 2
62-64 Ashes Pit 3
64-67 Ashes Pit 4
66-69 Burial 1
69-70 Burial Grounds (reach via Burial 2)
70-73 Burial 2
73-74 Burial 3
74-94 Burial 4

So after you hit 91 it gets hard to get exp... time for Drow and Papa Drow (fastest source for 9x imo and not as boring as Guildquest), but wait... yes right - time to train your skills before!
Depending on your party you might have to adjust tactic and will need different skills. Therefore it's handy to have the following.
Chaos III level 10 (should hit Black dragon ~70% of the time)
Hypnotize III level 10
Hypnotize IV level 9 (should hit Black dragon ~90% of the time)
Stun IV level 9 (makes last soulstone fight alot easier)

At this stage you also should be able to do Survival round 10 and Hurricane 9 (maybe 10) with decent equiment (see VI).
This way skilled level to 104, max Chaos I + II, Hypnotize I + II and Stun I + II and Seal up the remaining skills (note: trainer devices are bugged for poison, just in case you think about using them) and get ready for perfect reborn.

"Hint" do Soulstone Quest when you are 10x - it provides good experience.



IV Human skills

The knowledge in here is crucial to become a good Human - read it carefully.

Game Mechanics

Targets

In MWO you can only chose one target directly which will be hit by your mult-target spell. The rest of the remaining targets are chosen by factors you can't influence.
For female Humans are two factors you should know of:
1. Target's speed - affects Chaos, Hypnotize and Stun
2. Targets maximum HP - affects Poison

Example Drow:
- if I aim a Cleaverkiller with Chaos, only one Clever will be hit the rest "jumps" to Centauer Spirits.
- if I aim a Cenatauer Spirit with Poison, I will hit the targetted Centauer Spirit, then Drow, then the 3 Cleaver and if then more targets (according to skill level) are left (the case if Poison IV 10 or higher) I will hit Centauer Spirit(s)


Resistance

Normally resistance is capped at 100% against mage skills (95% against melee) for characters (90% for pets ) and an increase of that to 100% is only possible by Protect skill. But this rule doesn't apply to human skills.
Human skills are somehow (from my experience) based on probability. That means if I'm lucky I can encounter the very much unlikely case that I hit the Spirit Cat in Round 8 Survival with my Chaos IV level 7. On the other hand It can also happen that my Stun III level 9 fails on a crappy Capilla (as happened to me already).


Human Skills in detail

If anyone could provide me some data with skill lvls, targets, mp cost, duration I'd be very glad because I'm to lazy to not down during my skilling.

Sad as it is, Humans have to heavily rely on their III and IV skills. While a mages skill II is more or less as good as IV level 10, it doesn't work that way for humans (even Chaos II level 11 isn't as good as IV level IV)
All Human skills "overwrite" each other (except stun, which can't be "overwritten" anymore- you might see poisoned monsters in hypno, but that's just a display bug then.


Poison

Maybe the most tricky Skill...
- Poison always "jumps" to the target with highest maximum HP (as stated above).
- Human Poison and Pet Poison have some commons but also differences - be carefull comparing them!
- Poison damage consist of 2 "parts":
1. the initial damage that triggers when you successfully poison your opponent. The ammount of initial damage is determined by your character level and the level of your poison skill, but it's same no matter if you use I, II, III or IV. This initial damge is also not affected by Intelligence (opposite to pets).
2. the drain damage: this is a certain ammount of damage deducted from the poisoned target's HP whenever it gets a turn (lenght depending on skill / level of skill). This "drained" damage is influenced by the maximum HP of the poisoned targed (capped at 110k damage) and the skill you used. Poison I/II level x equals Poison III/IV level (x-2). If you compare skill I/III with II/IV at same level then I &gt; II and III&gt;IV. [I think that "drained damage isn't affected by Intelligence, but I never tested myself]
- Poison damage (in general) is not affected by perfect Human reborn bonus


Chaos

The most popular skill... (not much to say about)
- Chaos always "jumps" to the target with highest maximum speed
- Chaos puts enemy in an chaotic mode, so he neither can use skills nor differ between opponent and ally
- Chaos resistance lowers chance to get chaosed as well as the duration of it


Hypnotize
- Hypnotize always "jumps" to the target with highest maximum speed
- Hypnotize puts enemy in "speep", so he can't do anything other then potting others (not himself!) until duration runs out or gets hit by spell or attack (can be own pet / party member!)
- Hypnotize resistance lowers chance to get hypnotized as well as the duration of it
- Hypnotized enemies have 0% dodge! (secret feature f86)


Stun

- Stun always "jumps" to the target with highest maximum speed
- Stun freezes enemy so it neither receives nor can deal damage
- Stun resistance lowers chance to get stunned as well as the duration of it
- Stunned targets can be potted
- Ememy will always get a turn when moving out of stun (that's why it's mainly unpopular in GWs)



V Reborn

[If anyone reading this guide considers not being reborn at 104 and maxed skill I'll personally come to his server and smack him f86]
I see so often question about this, so here is the correct info:  
The reborn bonus in general consists of to parts:
1. the "normal" bonus
When you reborn you get a bonus to your resistance against Poison, Chaos, Hypnotize and Stun. This bonus will be 10% if all skills at level 10, less when you have lower skill-levels.
2. the "perfect" bonus
This one you'll get after you have been reborn with all skills maxed. Your targets will then -2% to all Human skills. I know it sounds not much, but you might notice it when meeting very high resistance monsters like Hurricane 14.  



VI Equipment

Luckily 2-2 build humans don't have to rely on equipment that heavily then full sta or full agi humans. Most time you'll do fine with essenced and incrusted +5 armor sets.  
Suggested armor sets (for beginners)
Always MP Necklace suggested, because you have high HP anyway and you get speed from essences.
Boots are something you are not allowed to be tight with money:   at least +10 with g10+ speed or dodge imbued (I prefer speed)
Physical set - +5 Helm/Armor/Necklace essenced with agi and incrusted with glimmer gems. This is your standard set, used for al purposes
Chaos set - +5 Helm/Armor/Necklace essenced with agi and incrusted with soul gems. (Partially) used at Papa / Survival / Hurricane / Guildwar
Stun set - +5 Helm/Armor/Necklace essenced with agi and incrusted with shadow gems. (Partially) used at Survival / Hurricane
Fire set - +5 Helm/Armor/Necklace essenced with agi and incrusted with black gems. (Partially) used at Survival / Hurricane

By the time you will of course become richer and be able to do more advanced sets using high grade gems.  



VII Pets

Another advantage of this build: pets don't matter. I took Igor (my TD) with me all the way from 40-104 never having the feeling "needing" another pet. But again I'll do a list for you.  
Must: Tank (shouldn't be too fast) with melee res (transfer GW egg on it and add melee crystals - add for 85+ after reborn) - Ae, Pede, TD, MR, Bee, ... whatever - you almost can't make a mistake here actually.
Should have: caster or attack-pet - just to name 2 cheap examples: TD + GW
Should have (later): Crit-pet - every pet with decent speed (100+ starting) and nice sta growth can be trained as critter
Optional: Speed tank with chaos res for special tasks like Survival / Hurricanes / Papa / GW - HC most popular



VIII Discussion

Sooooo, 2-2 sta-agi, eh?  
Reasons for this were plainly:
- highly flexible with equipment  
- not really caring about guildwar and humans reborn from cent -> human will always be faster and have more HP then a compareable human -> human  f15
- speed isn't everything at guildwar (skill and teamplay matters alot)
- cheap (see equipment and pets)
- high fun factor ( don't like dying or being chaosed all the time at quests)  

Don't get me wrong, I have nothing against insane high speed full agi humans. There's nothing I love more then having such a guy in my party at GW   , but I just couldn't play such a character (nor afford the equipment needed). And because I write guides from experience, I can't write a guide for Agihuman.

Last but not least, this guide is highly reflects my own experience / opinion - it doesn't have to fit to your opinion (if you have another or made different experiences with Humans).   


Hope this guide could help you.
Thanks for reading. Feel free to ask questions, add things, point out mistakes / typos (guess there are plenty of 'em - damn, I'm tired!  )
