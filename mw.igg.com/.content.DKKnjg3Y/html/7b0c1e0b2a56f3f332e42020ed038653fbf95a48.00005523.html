 Alas I had manage to complete the quest. Note, all the characters in my party are non reborn characters and I would say that it is not as hard as it first seem. About the comments from the writer and sparkblaze:

1. Yes, resistance speed up the process a whole lot. If your whole party have hypno resist for example, it takes 3-6 rounds to kill Worn General (Centaur) depending on your firepower. Chaos, Fire, Ice, Melee resist, Dodge sets come in very handy. As for Princess Agony, Frail resist but you must not forget about melee resist as well. As such, frail and dodge AOD if you are lucky enough to have one, will speed things up. Note: My party does not have any AOD and not all of us have melee/hypno/chaos/fire sets. We just adjust our char speeds so that we can absorb damage better and to make sure no one misses a turn. We do not have Deity/Saint or even HBs that can inflict crazy critical damages.

2. Showing off? If you think sharing information is showing off, then you should not read most of the content in the Experience Discussion forum. What was written here, firstly get me interested in fighting and experiment on ways of dealing with Worn Deity. After a few failed attempts and readjusting of strategies, my and my party had managed to pull things through. Most of the information here are accurate. I do not really agree with some of the strategies here though. My party only has 1 human and for some easier generals, no human at all (the reason being that my brother's human does not need experience any more)

3. Dark knight. For this mob, it is highly unlikely that your party will be killed. They have mediocre attacking power. If a party die, it is mostly because of "friendly fire". As such, do not use your heavy attacking pets, unless you are sure that they cannot be chaosed. If your party have chaos resist, you can fly through this in 3-5 rounds. Most of my party members do not have chaos resist. My strategy is simple. Just my M cent have chaos resist and uses purge II, making sure the made and other attacking chars is slower but faster than pets(can even disable pets) and well, can even AFK in the fight.

4. Drowlord is easier and better rewards? Well, for the first few rounds, yes as you will only get about 100k xp for each fight but once you reach round 5, it is whooping and the chaos/hypno generals are easy experience. Furthermore, Drowlord to not have a chance of AOD drop. I have yet to hear someone winning any AOD from Worn Deity though. Hahaha, maybe because nobody bothers trying.

5. Princess Agony. Well, the hot sub topic in this topic. The best equipment for this round is dodge AOD. With it, you are virtually sure of being able to survive. If not, get dodge shoe/necklace and melee resist set. Note maximum for melee resist is 95%. It is still quite tough as Princess cast frail and without being able to cast spells, you are virtually sitting ducks. As such strategy is very important. My suggestion is to kill agony first. Once she is gone and the frail effect wear off, the rest are quite simple.

 I did this a little the other day so I'll share a few things not mentioned.

There are 5 fights, and 5 rounds. You kill all 5 monster groups once per round. The xp seemed to go up 10k per kill for me.

I got to round 3. I was doing the human/chaos fight and my pet was chaosed. My pet ended up 1 shoting me(I was leader) right before the very last mob died. So I got a free tele out of it. Whats worse is that it reset my round status back to the very beginning. So try and be careful that your leader stays alive for these fights.

When I did princess I just killed her in the first or second round with a few crit pets. I cast stun 3 on the two fish and just saved them for last. With the princess dying right away, and the fish taken out the skeletons aren't too hard. Just be prepared for heavy melee damage from them. I won't comment on the other fights since people seem to be able to do them ok.

Overall I thought the quest was pretty much crap. Drow lord is much faster xp. What really slowed down worn diety was that all the fights ended up with a large amount of casting animation from the monsters. Combine that with heavy hypno and chaos so the fights took longer. Each worn diety fight ended up being much slower than what one drow lord fight takes me, and for worse xp.
