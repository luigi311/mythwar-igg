SEAL QUEST LEADERS

How to lead a Seal Party.

First off I am posting this because I couldn't find one in the forum. If someone has one already I'm sorry, not trying to step on any toes. Second, It took me a while to get up the courage to lead a Seal party because I didn't know what to do. This will hopefully help anyone who wants to lead. For you who are leading and have a hard time keeping a party because you don't know the following or can't remember what teleport scrolls to use hopefully this will help you out.

My advice, copy and paste this then print it out, or Write it down and keep it near the keyboard as an easy referance. Otherwise use your photographic memory. ( we all know the party leader always has a photographic memory and never forgets anything, LOL)

Clean out your inventory and bank what ya don't need, it will allow more room for tele's and fruit. (you need at least one spot open for the Seal scroll, so don't fill up)

Here's where the tele's take you:

    Blue (Wasteland Maps):     Flamingor
                                             Devil's Gate
                                             Cursed Abyss
                                             Flame Ruins

    Green (Desolate Maps):    Sunset Plains (easier to just walk there from Bar)
                                            Outcast City
                                            Badlands
                                            Bone Desert

           Red (City Maps):       Blython (go home quick after battle)
                                           Sewer2
                                           Demon Square
                                           Desert City

All Green (Designed Dungeons):    Tree of Life 3
                                                      Wasteland 2
                                                      Ashes Pit 2
                                                      Burial 3

       Blython Teleporter:    (not a tele scroll but you will need to use the Tele for Seal)
                                                      Wasteland 1
                                                      Ashes Pit 1
                                                      Burial 1

Stock up on Devil's Tears, you don't wanna waste time with mobs when most likely you won't get much for experience. I usually start out with 30 of 'em. It only takes up one inventory slot and your sure to have plenty.

Last but not least your gonna need Gold! Unlike the Defender Quests, Seal isn't free. You'll have to pay 250 for each scroll. plus you have to buy all the tele's and tears. This is the main reason you need to plan ahead in order to be a good Seal Party leader.

Now your ready to lead, get your party and head to the bar. Talk to Enderolth in the bar and buy a scroll. It will tell you what map and the coordinates on that map. Tele to the correct map and open your inventory. Use the purple seal scroll for the coordinates if you forget them. Use a tear so you can walk to the coordinates. Press tab for the map. Locate your coordinates and go get there.

Once the battle is done, BEFORE you tele back to Blython, MOVE. Just a couple steps. This insures that all the party members have exited the battle map. It also insures that no one gets disconected while teleporting. We all know there is "minimal LAG" *exteme sarcasm* in the game now so be considerate of your party members and don't leave 'em behind.

Now that your back in Blython, repeat! Have fun! And above all .... Keel them! LOL Hope this helps.

i think i saw loonnnng time ago a guide about seal somewhere but this one here takes a different approach.

nice tip about the walk a bit after battle, tho to clarify for newbies, the party leader should walk a bit AND see that others move with you. usually if someone in party gets stuck, the leader will see himself move but not members.

if you really want to be an outstanding seal leader, it's also a good idea to know roughly where the spot is on the map. this makes a difference in many places.

for example, sunsets.. there are 3 ways to get there for seals: (1) bly north-west exit (2) bly tele to waste 1 then walk outside (3) use light blue tele (= what you call green above). for each coordinate, one choice is usually better than the others.

another one is wasteland 1, you can get there from bly tele, or by teleport to wasteland 2 and walk to wasteland 1. these two ways come out of the two opposing entrances to wasteland 1.

yet another one is cursed abyss.... it's a ugly long winding big map and getting from right entrance helps a lot. if you get a coordinate close to lower-left, then group tele to bone and walk up is usually a much better choice.

i like buying scrolls in 2 times per full round. having 10 scrolls roughly after buying each time. 5 red to tele back, and 5 to go to the spots. there aren't many possible maps for each round.. for the first five rounds, usually 5 light blue. rarely you get one dark blue on the 5th round. so i always carry one extra dark blue just in case. then for the latter five rounds, i usually buy so that i have 4 dark blue and 3 dark green. you will need about 3/4 of your inventory (one page) for this, but it's quite systematic you might like it.

i also like to talk to the NPC first thing when you return to bar, allowing your members to leave party to heal and rejoin while you click click click. by the time you done clicking, accept the members back, talk to healer if you need, then tele out.

oh and last thing many newbies don't know is you can be pretty far off from the coordinate. like +/- 10 at least, so there is no need to position your character too nicely. just engage fast

if you are fast paced seal leader, people will like you.. or at least like to use you.
