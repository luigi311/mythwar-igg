  Mall Point Sale Scams
For all the players of California server, this post is regarding the series of recent scams by players posing as “Mall Point” sellers

At least 5 people were scammed by a person called BLANK who scammed people by giving them non-working codes in the name of selling Bags-Of-Magic. We all know how difficult it is to find Item mall sellers in California and what the market prices are. Please exclude the souls of sgt(edited by sliverat) and Noeldor, but they are winning with their over prized mall items anyway. This just forces us to buy mall items from people we have never heard of and thus trade with people we are forced to trust.

We all know that the GMs can’t do a thing about the items/money the people got scammed for. There is no compensation offered by IGG in case of any scams. They will say that you have to buy it from your own pocket. But not everyone has access to a credit card. So there is no way for them to get Mall items that can’t be traded normally without trusting Item-Mall sellers. Just makes life easy for those scammers.

I’m pretty sure that someone will say that it is up to the buyer to be careful in such transactions, but when there is something that is so much repetitive with all the preliminary warnings it just rings a bell on “What will be done to the scammers?” Again nothing will be done against those scammers because there is no proof.

So to all the players who buy Mall points, please get hold of something that can later be put up as proof.
1) Get to know the name of the player who is selling the item by adding him first.
2) Ask the players in your guild and your friends in the seller’s guild if he can be trusted.
3) Before giving the money make sure you finalize what you want and what you are paying in local chat and take a screenshot of the same.
4) Always make your payments after you get the system message “The item is now in your inventory”. Be sure to pay before you logoff .
5) Last but not least, please use the video recording option to capture all transactions.

Finally just because a player is high leveled doesn’t mean he can’t scam or you can trust him blindly. Make sure you have some friends/trustworthy people with you when you perform these transactions.

To all the sellers the same set of rules applies for you too as there is a chance the buyer might logoff before paying you the money. I’m not against trust-trading but misplaced trust is a pain in the butt. Say no to scamming .

Finally can sentinels do anything for scamming reports if proof is provided for the same including banning the scammers main accounts .
