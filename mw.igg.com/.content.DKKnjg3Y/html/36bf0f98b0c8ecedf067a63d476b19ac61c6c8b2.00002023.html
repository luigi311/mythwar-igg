decode macro error (length) and more

I'm sure many others have posted something to this as well, however. I'll put my 2 cents in.

1. On Many Occasions, I get the "decode macro error (length)" Error.  
   On the Mall Promoter, taking the Survey/Quiz for money.  I admit, this one has gotten better.  Instead of, like orginally, giving the Error every other "Question" it now only does it every 4-6 questions.  However, giving the nature of the Quiz/Survey, this is quite annoying.
   On the "New Horizon" (AKA Cesar Quest), at first, the quest right before the "Theif Quest" could not be finished, Qudos to the Techs for fixing that.  However, you can't get any further than the thief quest now.
   The Long and the Short of it is this...  We need to be sure the Quests are fixed.  If they are not fixed, turn that respective quest off.  I mean this is a beta,  It's better, in my opinion, to shut off a quest and have the NPC say something like "That Quest is Unavailable right now" rather than accepting the quest, going all over creation, using mass amounts of money/pots/time only to find out you can't claim your reward (EXP/Gold/Etc) because of "decode macro error (length)".
   
2. I would suggest that the GMs/Techs browse the NPCs Talking scripts.... I can't remember exactly where right now, but there are many places where an NPC will send you to a MW1 location (where it is on a different map or that place is non-existent in MW2).

3. All the pets by the Lottery/Awarder (Blython) seem to be miss labeled.

4. When in Quests, the Quest window, all too often, cuts off the end of the quest description... That is It is supposed to say something like "Bring (This Item) to (This Person) at (This Map AND/OR Coordinates)"  and instead it will say something like "Bring (This Item) to (This "  Essentially cutting off anywhere from the the middle of the NPC's name that we are trying to find to the coordinates.  As something in the end of the sentance might be crucial to you finishing a quest, and you probably have forgotten because you went to school, work, etc, this needs to be fixed ASAP (Thanks).

138# A A A Post at 2008-7-17 07:35 AM  Show author

Bug

I grouped a lot of small bugs together under each different number, for obvious reasons you'll see when you read it. I know I've repeated some mentioned earlier, but I couldn't be bothered reading over the 14 pages of bugs that already exist again. I skipped over repeating those I could remember as already having been mentioned by others, but if the moderator would like me to try to compile a cleaned-up list of bugs currently reported by others for easy reference, send me a PM and something shiny.

Came across several other bugs while doing quests and battles, but I didn't write them down so forgot them, unfortunately.

1. Dream crafter NPC has two options, to upgrade the AoD, and to exchange it for one with no level/strength requirements- both of these options, once selected, lead to the next pop-up on screen being frozen- the game doesn't freeze, but the pop-up 'sticks'- nothing the character can do can move onto any next step or close the pop-up.
The player also cannot log out to escape this screen, and has to force the the client to shut down.
2. Devil's tears effect disappear when the player changes server. As this is a mall item, this is quite annoying, and it is a 'bug' I'd like a direct answer on- if it is a bug, or if it is deliberately like that?
3. Many of the teleport destinations on the group teleport scrolls are mismarked- they take you to the wrong map.
On the dark blue teleport scroll, only one is marked correctly- 'Flamingor' teleports to Fog Jungle, Devil's gate is correct, Devil Lair takes you to Flame Ruins, Flame Ruins takes you to Cursed Abyss.
On the light blue/green teleport scroll, Sunset Plains takes you to Southern Rivage,  Outcast City to Bone Desert, Badlands is correct, Bone Desert takes you to Flamingor.
On the dark green teleport scroll: Sunset Plains takes you to Demon Lair 3, Wasteland 3 takes you to Evil lair 3, Ashes Pit 2 teleport is correct, and Burial 4 teleport takes you to Tumulus 4. I think some NPCs/dialog boxes also incorrectly refer to Tumulus as Burial.
4. The NPC teleporters show area names in dark blue even after you are of a high enough level to go there. The teleport function works even though the names are in blue.
5. The 'raiders at the gate' robbers have a pop-up after the fight saying you gain 100 reputation, which you don't.
6. The server numbers as said by system, NPC messages (those that have to do with activities on server 2, such as the guld war registery and mine war registery on servers 1 and 3) and by the players' friend list are wrong- server 1 is written as server 2, server 2 is written as server 3, and server 3 is written as server 4.
7. If you leave guild headquarters in a party, those in your party, after logging off, will reappear at Guild HQ entrance, even if they logged off on a different map (e.g. I lead a party with one other person in it out of Guild HQ. The person who was in my party logs off, while in my party or even after leaving my party, while in Woodlinger [as an example], and now when they log in next time they appear at Blython Guild HQ entrance). The leader logs back in in the right place.
8. The 'Manager' NPC in Guild HQ has several bugs. "Check service rate" function talks about the three titans in Desert City (which aren't actually there) rather than showing the player's service rate. "Transfer service rate" brings up the screen asking for a g5 or less gem to be given to Manager to increase player's skill points. A second bug here is that the Manager accepts g5 and ABOVE gems for skill points, at the same rate (amount of skill points given by a g5 and g6 gem is the same, higher grades I haven't tested. A g4 or lower isn't accepted by the Manager.)
The Manager's "leave" option shows the service rate, and doesn't  exit dialog from the NPC. To stop talking to the Manager, the player has to select one of several of the other options, "activate guild quest", "check service rate" or "Transfer service points," each of which end the dialog with the NPC after their message.
9. There is no NPC (which the website calls 'Elder') to start the brotherhood quest in Woodlinger, which is a shame as some have spent mall points on roses and crystal balls to increase friendship points, only to find no NPC to start the quest from. This leaves the brotherhood function untested.
10. Clicking "leave" in the Blython Sentinel's first dialog box brings up the option to join the Master's Contest. There is no dialog option in the Sentinel's dialog to deliberately bring this up.
11. If you select 'I need forge' (Which should be 'I need to forge' for clear English) from the Smith Helper NPC, the screen for passing ores pops up. This is fine, but if you don't have the forging items or don't want to,  and cancel out of the 'give' screen, each time you click on him from then on the 'give' screen pops-up asking for the armour and ore to forge with. This means you can't use that NPC's other functions. This can be fixed by passing items to be forged to the NPC, which allows you to use his other functions (dialog options) again.
Also with this NPC, if you select dialog option 'Details' and then 'Dream equipments forge details' no dialog box comes up.
12. Enchanter NPC in Demon Square still asks for a rainbow stone and equipment even if you select the 'Exit' option.
13. Town guide NPC in Woodlinger- when you click the 'tell me more about rewards' option, no dialog box comes up.
His quest information on redeem points is extremely unclear in meaning, and he mentions NPCs as being marked on maps with a 'red dot' rather than a yellow diamond, as is the case.
This NPC also says quest information about the mokka quest: that it gives 80 skill points, when it gives 250.
Bounty quest information combines hellion and bounty quest into normal and advanced bounty hunt(which is fine) but then further down in the quest options is 'Hellion' which instead of giving Hellion information, talks about the Igor quest.
His quest information on crab catching gives the wrong days and possibly wrong times, as does the website.
This NPC says to go to Demon Square or Desert City to get crystals for pet resistance, instead of saying 'Evil Lair' or 'Demon Lair.'
14. Blython town guide: In his quests information in 'redeem points' option he calls the Elven Witch NPC 'Mcdonald'.
The times he says for the lottery event are in conflict with those stated on the website, I'm not sure which is correct.
15. Desert City town guide in 'Quests' gives the wrong co-ordinates for the Sleeping Dragon NPC.

What number am I up to?

16. The Lansquenet Official in Woodlinger and Lansquenet Master in Blython are referred to ingame by the quest log and also some dialog boxes as 'Mercenary Chief' and 'Mercenary Leader' respectively
17. A lot of NPC's give a 'decode macro error', including (but likely not limited to): Ore Tracker in server 2, the Wisdom NPC in the 'test for kingship' quest, Mall Promoter, the NPC in lost space (from the box that teleports you to a different map where you play jackpot to exit, not sure if that is the correct name).
*** Suggestion to the moderator: Create a more comprehensive list than I have here of bugs found and post it at the start of this topic or as a seperate topic on the forum, so not so many people repeat already found bugs. Those people (and those involved in fixing the bugs) can then read a condensed list of known bugs to aid in their bughunt/fix, and be warned what to avoid when playing. Delete from this created list of bugs each bug when it has been fixed (and confirmed to be fixed).   Keeping a list of bugs (seperately) on the forum that have already been fixed would be good for publicity. ***
Postscript: If guild information, friendlists, pet intimacy, and other alike things will be deleted when the open beta becomes fully released Myth Wars 2, mall points spent on items for the guild, to increase friendship and increase pet intimacy, should be automatically returned to the game account. Just a thought that I'm sure others will agree with.

Also a forum bug/inconvenience: By the time I'd finished writing all of this out, I'd been automatically logged off of the forum, so if I hadn't copy/pasted the information, I would have had to try to find all of these bugs and write them out again.
